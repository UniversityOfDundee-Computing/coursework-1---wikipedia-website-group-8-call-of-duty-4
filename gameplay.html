Gameplay

Sergeant John "Soap" MacTavish

Sergeant MacTavish, a recent SAS recruit, is a sniper and demolitions expert. Prior to serving with the SAS, MacTavish was a member of the 3rd Battalion, Parachute Regiment. He plays a key role as a frontline soldier against the Ultra-nationalists in Captain Price's Bravo Team.

Campaign

Call of Duty 4: Modern Warfare's campaign tasks the player with completing a series of missions as a selection of different characters, with each having a number of linear objectives. Some objectives require that the player arrives at a checkpoint, while others have the player eliminating enemies in an area, defending a location, or planting explosive charges on an enemy installation. Throughout the campaign players can collect pieces of intel which reward the player with game cheats and visual filters such as infinite ammunition, cluster grenades, and increased contrast.

Plot

In 2011, a civil war has broken out in Russia between the government and ultranationalists. Meanwhile, and anti-western seperatist group led by Khaled Al-Asad siezes power in a middle-eastern country. British SAS forces conducting operations in the area discover that Al-Asad is in possesion of russian nuclear devices.

Acting on this information, US forces launch an assault on Al-Asad's presidential palace however, in the fighting, the nuclear device detonates, destroying the palace and surrounding city. Asad survives the blast and is hunted down by Captain Price's SAS team revealing to them that the device was supplied by Imran Zakhaev, leader of the Russian ultranationalists. Price and his team attempt to capure Zakhaev's son, Victor, in order to learn his whereabouts. Refusing to be captured, Victor commits suicide, enraging Zakhaev.

In retaliation, he captures a nuclear launch facility and fires several nuclear missiles at the eastern US. The SAS and a US force breach the facility and manage to remotely destroy the missiles before confronting and killing Zakhaev. In the epilogue it is revealed that the events of the game are covered up, the consequences of which are the subject of the game's sequal, Call of Duty: Modern Warfare 2
